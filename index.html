About

Bradley Perkins is a programmer turned fictional drama and adventure author. After years of writing lines of code, he decided to go back to school and earn an MFA in Creative Writing. For him, writing fictional stories is more rewarding than writing the many lines of code for a program. His works consist of writing serious and gritty fictional stories with realistic tones. While writing his adventure stories, he always throws his hero into dangerous situations and keeps the audience in suspense. This is what excites him the most about writing adventure. By staying true to the classic elements, he creates relatable characters and compelling stories that the audience can get behind. With his serious and gritty approach to his more dramatic work, this is a way that he can keep the attention of his audience. His goal is to show that the dramatic genre is not only about the touching and heartfelt stories, but that it can also be used to tell stories that will excite. With his adventure stories, he wants to let people escape and feel like they are part of the adventure themselves.

He writes screenplays, comics, and short stories. He plans to eventually evolve from his short stories and into longer-form novels. By honing his skills in a shorter story format, he believes that this will allow him to create more compelling longer fictional novels. This format excites him because there is always a chance that either the short stories or novels could be adapted to a screenplay.

While earning his MFA in Creative Writing from Full Sail University, he was taught how to write for various media. He learned about character development, proper story-structure and the right ways to tell a compelling story.

Bradley lives in Ohio with his wife, three kids, two border collies, and comic book collection. He also collects and restores arcades from the ’80s and ’90s.
